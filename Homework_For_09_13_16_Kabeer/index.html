KNOW MORE ABOUTDYSLEXIA

It is not a disease, just a conginitive learning disability. Dyslexia, also known as reading disorder, is characterized by trouble with reading despite normal intelligence. Different people are affected to varying degrees. The difficulties are involuntary and people with this disorder have a normal desire to learn and can be cured easily.

Dyslexia is Curable.

Dyslexia is the most common learning disability, affecting 3–7% of the population; however, up to 20% may have some degree of symptoms. Treatment involves adjusting teaching methods to meet the person's needs. While not curing the underlying problem, it may decrease the degree of symptoms.

Problems may include difficulties in spelling words, reading quickly, writing words, "sounding out" words in the head, pronouncing words when reading aloud and understanding what one reads.

Sujjested Treatments & Methods for coping up with Dyslexia

Continous Learning

Through the use of compensation strategies, therapy and educational support, dyslexic individuals can learn to read and write. There are techniques and technical aids which help to manage or conceal symptoms of the disorder.

Hand-Eye-Ear Co-ordination

For dyslexia intervention with alphabet-writing systems, the fundamental aim is to increase a child's awareness of correspondences between graphemes (letters) and phonemes (sounds), and to relate these to reading and spelling by teaching how sounds blend into words.

Love and Support

Early intervention – that is done in children at a young age– can be successful in reducing reading failure. Removing stress and anxiety alone can sometimes improve written comprehension.

    There are various types of Dyslexia which effect various parts of the cognitive sytem in various degrees. Some of them are Surface dyslexia, Phonological dyslexia, Deep dyslexia, Pure dyslexia, Hemianopic dyslexia, Attentional dyslexia.


  • Ailments which are Similar to Dyslexia

    Dyslexia is often misdiagonosed by several learning disabilities, but it is unclear whether they share underlying neurological causes. These associated disabilities include Dysgraphia, Attention deficit hyperactivity disorder, Auditory processing disorder, Developmental coordination disorder
